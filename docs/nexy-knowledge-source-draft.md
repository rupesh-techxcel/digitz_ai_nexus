## SOURCE 1 — Nexy Companion — Overview and Intelligence Architecture

**Topic:** What Nexy Is and How the Intelligence Engine Works

---

Nexy is the Business Companion included in every Nexus Orbit plan. It is not a scripted chatbot or a question-and-answer widget. Nexy is an AI-powered conversation engine that runs a structured intelligence process on every message a visitor sends — before deciding what to say next.

Nexy's purpose is to understand what a visitor is trying to do, where they are in their decision journey, and what outcome they are working toward — and to guide the conversation toward a meaningful next step for the business.

### Three Intelligence Layers

Before Nexy forms any response, three independent assessment layers run simultaneously on every visitor message. Each layer answers a different question.

**Layer 1 — Controller Milestones**
What is Nexy working toward right now in this conversation? Nexy always has a specific goal — a milestone it is actively advancing toward. There are eight milestones covering the full arc from opening contact to confirmed conversion. Nexy does not jump milestones. It advances based on what the visitor has actually said and what discovery data has been gathered.

**Layer 2 — Journey Stage**
Where is the visitor in their own decision process? This is tracked separately from Nexy's milestone. Eleven stages describe where the visitor sits in the buying funnel — from first contact through to conversion. Journey stage tells Nexy how much the visitor already knows and how close they are to a decision.

**Layer 3 — Buying Signal**
How is the visitor behaving emotionally in this specific message? Fourteen signal types classify the visitor's current state — ranging from strong buying intent through to disengagement risk. The signal shapes Nexy's tone and the urgency of its response for that message.

All three layers are assessed independently and combined. The result determines what Nexy says, how directly it says it, and what action (if any) it takes.

### What Nexy Never Does

Nexy does not claim Nexus can help with a specific business problem unless approved knowledge explicitly confirms it. If a visitor describes a challenge, Nexy first understands the current state — what the visitor is doing today — before connecting it to any Nexus capability. Discovery comes before solution.

Nexy does not invent answers. Every factual claim about Nexus products, pricing, or capabilities is retrieved from the approved knowledge base. If no approved knowledge covers a question, Nexy says so and suggests a next step.

## SOURCE 2 — Nexy Companion — Milestones and Journey Stages

**Topic:** Conversation Milestones and Visitor Journey Stages

---

### The 8 Controller Milestones — What Nexy Is Working Toward

At any point in a conversation, Nexy is working toward one of eight milestones. These represent the natural progression from first contact to a confirmed outcome. Nexy advances through milestones based on what the visitor has said and what has been discovered.

**Open Contact**
The conversation has started. Nexy introduces itself and begins collecting context about who the visitor is and what they are looking for.

**Profile Building**
Nexy is gathering foundational information — the visitor's industry, company size, business context. This information builds the visitor's profile and feeds the persona matching process.

**Pain Surface**
A specific business challenge or pain point has emerged. Nexy explores the current state: what methodology, tool, or process is the visitor using today? This discovery comes before any Nexus solution is mentioned.

**Solution Fit**
The visitor's challenge has been understood. Nexy now connects it to confirmed Nexus capabilities — using only approved knowledge. No capabilities are implied or invented. Only what is documented and approved is stated.

**Interest Confirmed**
The visitor has expressed clear buying interest. Nexy begins discussing practical next steps — what a demonstration would look like, or what a consultation would cover.

**Demo Requested**
The visitor has asked to see the platform. Nexy confirms the demo request and arranges for a Nexus Consultant to follow up using the visitor's registered contact details.

**Escalated**
The visitor wants to speak with a person. Nexy manages the structured handover to a desk agent, with all conversation context and visitor profile transferred.

**Converted**
A concrete next step has been confirmed — a demo request submitted, a consultation arranged, or a Nexus Contact created. The business has a qualified record with everything gathered during the conversation.

---

### The 11 Journey Stages — Where the Visitor Is

Journey stage is assessed separately from Nexy's milestone. It tracks where the visitor sits in their own decision-making process — independent of what Nexy is working toward.

**Opening Contact**
The first message has been received. No profile information has been gathered yet. Nexy begins from a neutral position.

**Profile Building**
Basic business context is being collected — what the visitor does, their industry, their team.

**Pain Discovery**
A specific challenge has surfaced. Nexy is exploring it — understanding the current approach and the gap the visitor is trying to close.

**Exploring Options**
The visitor is considering approaches, asking what is possible, or comparing alternatives.

**Understanding Value**
The visitor is assessing whether Nexus fits their specific situation and what the outcome could be for their business.

**Objection Handling**
A concern or hesitation has emerged. Nexy addresses it directly while maintaining the discovery thread.

**Interest Confirmed**
The visitor has expressed clear intent to move forward. The conversation is now oriented toward next steps.

**Demo Requested**
The visitor has asked for a demonstration of the platform.

**Proposal Stage**
Specific requirements are being discussed — what the implementation would look like, which plan fits, what is included.

**Escalated**
A human agent has joined the conversation. The AI phase of the journey is complete.

**Converted**
A confirmed next step has been agreed and recorded. The visitor is now a qualified lead in the system.

---

Journey stage and milestone are updated after every message. The combination of the two tells the desk team exactly where a visitor was in their journey when they escalated or converted — without reading the full transcript.

## SOURCE 3 — Nexy Companion — Buying Signals

**Topic:** Buying Signal Classification and What Each Signal Means

---

### What Is a Buying Signal?

A buying signal is Nexy's classification of how the visitor is behaving in a specific message — their emotional and decision-making state right now, not across the whole conversation. Fourteen signal types are used. The signal is assessed on every message and can change from one message to the next.

The signal does three things: it shapes Nexy's tone for that response, it informs whether Nexy should advance or hold back, and it contributes to the visitor's journey stage progression when the signal is strong enough.

---

### Strong Buying Intent Signals

These signals indicate the visitor is moving toward a decision. Nexy responds with directness and focuses on next steps.

**Price Question** — The visitor is asking about cost, pricing, plans, or what is included. This is a strong forward signal — visitors who ask about price are actively evaluating.

**Demo Request** — The visitor wants to see the platform in action. Nexy confirms the request and prepares the handover to a Nexus Consultant.

**Next Step Inquiry** — The visitor is asking what happens next or how they proceed. Nexy outlines the path forward clearly.

**Buy Intent** — The visitor has expressed intention to get started, sign up, or move forward. Nexy moves to confirm the next action.

---

### Soft Interest Signals

These signals indicate genuine interest without a clear commitment. Nexy continues discovery and presents relevant information.

**Capability Question** — The visitor is asking what Nexus can do for a specific scenario. Nexy answers from approved knowledge only and avoids implying capabilities that are not confirmed.

**Comparison Question** — The visitor is comparing Nexus with alternatives or asking how it differs. Nexy focuses on what Nexus specifically does rather than comparing against competitors.

**General Interest** — The visitor is engaged and curious but has not focused on a specific outcome yet. Nexy continues building context.

---

### Friction and Resistance Signals

These signals indicate hesitation or a barrier. Nexy addresses the friction directly without abandoning the conversation thread.

**Objection** — The visitor has raised a concern about Nexus, the process, or the fit. Nexy acknowledges it and responds with relevant information.

**Price Resistance** — The visitor has indicated price is a concern. Nexy does not discount — it focuses on understanding the scope and finding the right fit.

**Competitor Preference** — The visitor has mentioned an alternative they are already using or considering. Nexy acknowledges and continues exploring the specific challenge rather than competing directly.

**Time Stall** — The visitor is indicating this is not the right time. Nexy holds the relationship open without pushing.

---

### Disengagement Risk Signals

These signals indicate the visitor may leave or has lost the thread. Nexy attempts re-engagement or moves toward a graceful close.

**Off-Topic** — The visitor has gone to a subject outside the conversation scope. Nexy responds briefly and steers back to the primary thread.

**Repeated Question** — The visitor is asking something they have already asked, which may indicate the previous answer was unclear. Nexy responds differently and checks whether the gap has been addressed.

**Low Engagement** — Short, non-committal responses. Nexy shifts approach — asks a simpler question or offers a concrete next step to re-anchor the visitor.

**Goodbye** — The visitor is ending the conversation. Nexy closes gracefully, offers a contact path, and ensures the session record is complete.

---

Three or more consecutive resistance or disengagement signals trigger escalation readiness — Nexy recognises that the conversation has stalled and that a human may be better placed to continue.

## SOURCE 4 — Nexy Companion — Visitor Profiling and Persona Matching

**Topic:** How Nexy Builds a Visitor Profile and Matches Customer Archetypes

---

### What Is Persona Matching?

As Nexy converses with a visitor, it builds a structured profile of who that visitor is — not just what they are asking, but what kind of business they represent, what challenges they face, and what they are trying to achieve.

This profile is matched against a set of customer archetypes — called Nexus Companion Personas — that the business has configured in advance. A persona is a named profile of a typical customer: for example, "Growing Services Business", "Owner-Managed Retail", or "Enterprise Operations Team".

When a visitor's emerging profile matches one of these archetypes, Nexy knows which products and services are most likely to be relevant, which challenges are common to that type of buyer, and how direct or detailed to be in its responses.

---

### How the Matching Works

The match uses weighted keyword scoring across six discovery fields. Each field has a weight that reflects how reliably it identifies the persona type.

| Discovery Field | Weight | What It Captures |
|---|---|---|
| Industry | 30% | The sector the visitor's business operates in |
| Company Size | 20% | Team size, headcount, or operational scale |
| Challenges | 20% | Business problems the visitor has described |
| Pain Points | 15% | Specific friction areas the visitor has mentioned |
| Business Maturity | 10% | Whether the business is a startup, growing, or established |
| Goals | 5% | What the visitor is trying to achieve |

Each configured persona has its own keyword profiles for these fields. As the visitor shares information, Nexy scores the visitor's profile against all configured personas. The persona with the highest matching score above the minimum threshold is considered matched.

The minimum threshold to count as a match is 20 out of 100. Below that, no persona is assigned and Nexy continues in general discovery mode.

---

### Continuous Re-Matching

Persona matching does not happen once and lock in. It runs after every visitor message, re-scored against the accumulated profile. As the visitor shares more, the match can:

- Strengthen — existing persona becomes more confident
- Switch — a different persona scores higher as more context emerges
- Appear for the first time — enough profile data has accumulated to cross the threshold

This means Nexy's understanding of the visitor sharpens throughout the conversation rather than being guessed at from the first message.

---

### What a Matched Persona Enables

**More targeted recommendations.** Each Nexus product and service can be associated with specific personas. When a persona is matched, Nexy prioritises the options most likely to resonate with that visitor type.

**Higher enquiry score.** Persona confidence contributes up to 30 points of the 0–100 enquiry score. A visitor who strongly matches a configured persona is rated as more qualified — because Nexy knows which solution applies to them.

**Richer handover information.** When the conversation escalates to a desk agent or converts to a Nexus Contact, the matched persona and confidence score are carried across. The desk team immediately knows what type of customer this visitor is, without reading the full transcript.

---

### What Personas Are Configured By

Each Nexus Companion Persona record contains:

- **Persona name** — the archetype label used internally
- **Industry** — the sectors this type of customer typically operates in
- **Customer type** — B2B, B2C, Government, Non-Profit, and similar
- **Company size** — typical team size or headcount range
- **Business maturity** — Startup, Growing, Established, or Enterprise
- **Challenges** — the common problems this type of business faces
- **Pain points** — specific friction areas common to this persona
- **Goals** — what this type of customer is typically trying to achieve
- **Desired outcomes** — the results this persona is looking for

The richer and more specific these fields are, the more accurately Nexy can match visitors to the right archetype during a live conversation.

## SOURCE 5 — Nexy Companion — Enquiry Scoring

**Topic:** How Conversations Are Scored and What the Score Drives

---

### What Is the Enquiry Score?

Every conversation Nexy handles produces a live score from 0 to 100. The enquiry score represents how qualified and ready for a next step the visitor is — based on everything Nexy has gathered during the conversation.

The score is recalculated after every visitor message and is visible to desk operators in real time on the Nexus Live console.

---

### How the Score Is Calculated

The score is a composite of three components:

**Discovery Completeness — up to 40 points**
How much of the visitor's profile has been gathered. The more context Nexy has collected — industry, company size, challenges, goals — the higher this component. A visitor who has shared nothing scores 0 here. A visitor who has shared their full business context scores near 40.

**Persona Confidence — up to 30 points**
How strongly the visitor matches one of the business's configured customer archetypes. A strong match (confidence 90+) contributes close to 30 points. No match contributes 0. This component rewards visitors whose profile clearly fits a known customer type.

**Qualification Signals — up to 30 points**
The presence of specific buying signal keywords in the conversation. Phrases that indicate readiness — asking about pricing, requesting a demo, expressing intent to move forward — contribute to this component. More and stronger signals mean a higher score.

Total: 100 points.

---

### What the Score Drives

**Escalation readiness.** The playbook for each Nexy configuration includes a score threshold. When the enquiry score crosses that threshold and the visitor has shown buying signals, Nexy moves toward offering a human handover or demo confirmation as the natural next step.

**Prioritisation on the desk.** Desk agents see the enquiry score on every escalated conversation. A score of 75+ indicates a highly qualified visitor with a clear need and buying intent. A score of 30 indicates early-stage discovery. Agents can prioritise accordingly.

**Nexus Contact quality.** When a visitor converts or escalates, the final enquiry score is carried into the Nexus Contact record. Over time, the business can identify which score ranges convert to customers — and tune their playbook thresholds accordingly.

**Persona-driven routing (future).** High-confidence persona matches combined with high enquiry scores can be routed to specialist consultants automatically — the right person for the right type of customer, based on objective scoring.

---

### What a Score Means in Practice

| Score Range | What It Typically Indicates |
|---|---|
| 0–20 | Opening contact. Little profile information gathered. No signals yet. |
| 20–40 | Early discovery. Some context established. No strong buying signals. |
| 40–60 | Active exploration. Profile is building. Interest signals present. |
| 60–75 | Qualified visitor. Clear challenge identified. Persona matched. Interest confirmed. |
| 75–90 | High-quality lead. Strong buying signals. Ready for human conversation or demo. |
| 90–100 | Highly qualified. Full profile, strong persona match, explicit buying intent. Priority escalation. |

## SOURCE 6 — Nexy Companion — Human Escalation, Desk Handoff and Meeting Booking

**Topic:** Escalation Flow, Desk Handoff, and Direct Meeting Scheduling

---

### When Nexy Hands Over to a Person

Nexy handles the full discovery conversation — but when a visitor is ready to speak with a consultant, Nexy manages a structured handover that carries everything that has been gathered.

Escalation is triggered in two ways:

**Visitor-initiated.** The visitor explicitly asks to speak with a consultant, a person, or a human agent. Nexy recognises the request immediately and begins the handover process.

**Score-triggered.** When the visitor's enquiry score crosses the threshold set in the playbook, and the visitor has shown consistent buying signals, Nexy offers escalation as the natural next step rather than continuing the AI conversation.

Escalation is not a fallback for when AI cannot answer. It is a designed outcome — the point where a qualified visitor with a confirmed profile is connected to a person who can advance the relationship.

---

### The Escalation Flow — Step by Step

**Step 1 — Email Verification**

If the visitor does not yet have a verified email on file, Nexy asks for it before proceeding. A one-time verification code is sent to the address provided. The visitor enters the code in the chat window. This step ensures every escalated visitor has a confirmed contact address — the desk team never receives an uncontactable lead.

If the visitor's email is already verified from an earlier point in the conversation, this step is skipped.

**Step 2 — Escalation Record Created**

Once email is verified, Nexy creates a structured escalation record. This record contains:
- The visitor's verified email and phone
- The full conversation transcript
- The enquiry score at the moment of escalation
- The matched customer persona and confidence score
- All discovery data gathered — industry, company size, challenges, goals

**Step 3 — Desk Team Notified**

The Nexus Live desk console receives a real-time alert. Desk agents see the new escalation, can review the visitor profile and conversation summary, and can accept the handover.

**Step 4 — Agent Accepts and Joins**

When a desk agent accepts the escalation, they join the active conversation. The visitor is notified. The agent has the full context — transcript, visitor profile, enquiry score — without the visitor needing to repeat anything.

---

### Book a Meeting — Direct Scheduling Without Waiting

When escalation is initiated, the visitor does not have to wait passively for an agent to become available.

If the Nexus AI Agent Profile is configured with a meeting scheduling link, Nexy presents a **Book a Meeting** card directly in the chat window at the moment of escalation.

The card shows:
- A title: **Book a Meeting**
- A description: *Schedule a time directly with our team at your convenience.*
- A direct button: **Book a Meeting →**

The visitor can self-schedule immediately — in the same chat session, without leaving the window, without a separate form or email. The scheduled meeting and the escalation record exist side by side. The desk team can see both.

The message Nexy sends when a meeting link is available:
*"Your request has been forwarded to our team — an agent will respond based on their availability. Alternatively, you can book a meeting directly using the calendar below."*

Different Nexy configurations can carry different meeting links. A Business Companion conversation can point to a sales consultant calendar. A Customer Support conversation can point to a support specialist calendar. The visitor always reaches the right type of person.

---

### What the Desk Team Receives

When a desk agent opens an escalated conversation, they have immediate access to:

| Information | Source |
|---|---|
| Full conversation transcript | Every message from visitor and Nexy |
| Visitor contact details | Name, verified email, phone |
| Matched customer persona | Which archetype the visitor matched |
| Persona confidence | How strongly they matched (0–100) |
| Enquiry score | Overall qualification score at escalation |
| Discovery data | Industry, company size, challenges, goals |
| Buying signals detected | Which signal types were classified |
| Journey stage at escalation | Where the visitor was in the decision funnel |
| Milestone at escalation | What Nexy was working toward when handover occurred |

The agent does not start from zero. The entire Nexy discovery session is pre-context for the human conversation.

---

### Auto-Promotion to Nexus Contact

When a conversation reaches Converted or Escalated status, Nexus automatically creates or updates a **Nexus Contact** record carrying the visitor's verified details, matched persona, persona confidence, enquiry score, and all discovery data. This becomes the starting point for any CRM follow-up or outreach workflow in the business's Nexus environment.

---

### The Visitor Experience — End to End

From the visitor's perspective:

1. They ask to speak with someone, or Nexy offers it as the natural next step
2. If email is needed, they receive a quick verification code in the chat
3. Once verified, Nexy confirms: *"Your request has been forwarded to our team"*
4. A **Book a Meeting** card appears if a scheduling link is configured — they can schedule now
5. When a desk agent accepts, the visitor is notified and the live conversation continues with the human

The visitor never leaves the chat window. There is no redirect, no separate form, no email chain. The full journey — from Nexy conversation to human consultation — happens in a single interface.

## SOURCE 7 — Nexy Companion — AI Personas, Playbooks and Grounded Knowledge

**Topic:** Persona Configuration, Playbooks, Plan Limits, and Knowledge Governance

---

### AI Personas — Distinct Versions of Nexy

An AI Persona is a named, behaviourally distinct version of Nexy. Each persona has its own name, tone, behavior instructions, welcome message, and discovery style. A business might configure one persona for public website visitors using a warm, conversational approach and a second persona for returning customers using a more concise, action-oriented style.

Personas are independent of customer archetypes (Nexus Companion Personas). AI Personas define how Nexy behaves. Customer archetypes define what type of visitor Nexy is talking to.

| Plan | AI Personas |
|---|---|
| Orbit Lite | 2 |
| Orbit | 5 |
| Orbit Pro | 10 |
| Orbit Command | Unlimited |

---

### Conversation Playbooks — Structured Conversation Flows

A playbook is a structured conversation guide that Nexy follows for a specific scenario. It defines the sequence of discovery questions, the information to present at each stage, the conditions that advance the conversation, the escalation score threshold, and the milestone targets.

Common playbooks include: product enquiry, demo request, objection handling, and escalation to human. Each playbook is matched to a specific Nexy AI Persona, ensuring that the conversation structure fits the audience and the goal.

| Plan | Playbooks |
|---|---|
| Orbit Lite | 2 |
| Orbit | 4 |
| Orbit Pro | 6 |
| Orbit Command | Unlimited |

---

### Grounded Knowledge — Nexy Does Not Guess

Every factual statement Nexy makes about Nexus capabilities, products, pricing, or processes is retrieved from the approved knowledge base at query time. Nexy does not draw on general AI training data for business-specific answers.

Before answering any question about what Nexus can do, Nexy retrieves the relevant approved content and uses that as its source. If no approved knowledge covers a visitor's question, Nexy acknowledges the gap clearly and suggests a next step — typically offering to connect the visitor with a consultant who can answer directly.

This means businesses have precise control over what Nexy says about their offering. Nothing that is not in the knowledge base will be stated as fact. Knowledge is managed, reviewed, and published through Nexus Studio before it becomes available to Nexy in a live conversation.

---

### What Stays Consistent Across All Plans

The platform capability is identical across Orbit Lite, Orbit, Orbit Pro, and Orbit Command. All plans include:
- The full three-layer intelligence engine (Milestones, Journey Stages, Buying Signals)
- Persona matching against configured customer archetypes
- Enquiry scoring (0–100 composite)
- Human escalation with email verification
- Meeting booking card (when scheduling link is configured)
- Auto-promotion to Nexus Contact on conversion
- Grounded knowledge retrieval with citation support
- Real-time desk monitoring and agent handover

What varies between plans is the number of AI Personas, Playbooks, and the monthly conversation volume included.

## SOURCE 8 — Nexy Companion — Communication Channels

**Topic:** Website AI Chat and WhatsApp Outreach — Where Nexy Operates

---

### Where Nexy Runs

Nexy is not limited to a single channel. The same intelligence engine, the same knowledge base, the same personas, playbooks, and escalation flow operate across both channels that Nexy currently supports.

Regardless of where a visitor starts their conversation — website or WhatsApp — they receive the same Nexy experience: three-layer intelligence, persona matching, enquiry scoring, and human escalation when ready.

---

### Channel 1 — Website AI Chat

The Website AI Chat is the primary Nexy channel. Nexy is embedded as a chat widget on the business's website through the Nexus Website Widget — a lightweight component linked to a Nexus Live Channel.

**How the website chat starts:**
When a visitor opens the chat widget, they are presented with available conversation categories. For visitors who want to engage with Nexy, the Nexy Companion option initiates the Business Companion session. The visitor's category selection is the entry point — from that moment Nexy's companion engine is active for the session.

**What the website chat widget supports:**
- Full Nexy companion conversation — milestones, journey stages, buying signals
- Persona matching and enquiry scoring running in real time
- Human escalation with email verification — all within the widget
- **Book a Meeting card** — presented inline when escalation is requested and a scheduling link is configured
- Identity-based routing — public visitors and registered visitors can receive different experiences and knowledge access through the same widget
- Demo request confirmation — Nexy confirms the request and sets the handover in motion

**The visitor experience:**
The visitor interacts through a clean chat interface on the website. All Nexy activity happens within this widget. The visitor never needs to leave the page, complete a separate form, or send an email to initiate a consultation.

---

### Channel 2 — WhatsApp Outreach

WhatsApp Outreach extends the Nexy companion framework to WhatsApp. The same knowledge base, personas, playbooks, and escalation configuration used on the website apply directly to WhatsApp conversations — no separate setup required.

**How WhatsApp conversations start:**
When a visitor contacts the business's WhatsApp Business number, the message is routed through the Nexus Live pipeline automatically. Nexy responds as the Business Companion using the same intelligence engine as the website chat. There is no separate WhatsApp-specific training or persona configuration needed.

**What WhatsApp conversations include:**
- Full three-layer intelligence: milestones, journey stages, buying signals — identical to website chat
- All configured personas and playbooks apply
- Enquiry scoring runs on every WhatsApp message
- Conversation logs are recorded and visible on the Nexus Live desk console
- Human escalation is available — a desk agent can accept and join the WhatsApp conversation
- Image, audio, video, and document messages are received and noted in the conversation record

**WhatsApp message capacity by plan:**

| Plan | Monthly Messages | Overage |
|---|---|---|
| Orbit Lite | 2,000 | +$25 per additional 2,000 |
| Orbit | 5,000 | +$25 per additional 2,000 |
| Orbit Pro | 12,000 | No overage — included |
| Orbit Command | Unlimited | No overage |

Each individual message sent or received counts toward the monthly capacity. A conversation of 10 exchanges counts as 10 messages.

Orbit Pro removes per-message uncertainty entirely. Businesses expecting more than 5,000 WhatsApp messages per month are recommended to start on Orbit Pro.

**WhatsApp setup:**
WhatsApp Outreach is included in all Nexus Orbit plans at no additional charge. The WhatsApp Business account integration is configured during the onboarding process. No separate subscription is needed.
